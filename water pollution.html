    • Water pollution

    Water pollution refers to the contamination of water bodies such as rivers, lakes, oceans, and groundwater. It occurs when harmful substances, including chemicals, waste, and pathogens, are introduced into these water sources. This pollution can have severe impacts on aquatic ecosystems, human health, and the environment. Common sources of water pollution include industrial discharges, agricultural runoff, sewage disposal, and plastic waste.

    Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies like lakes, rivers, oceans, aquifers and groundwater. Water pollution occurs when pollutants are discharged directly or indirectly into water bodies without adequate treatment to remove harmful compounds.
